I am a Postdoctoral Associate at MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL), working with Professors Jonathan Ragan-Kelley and Justin Solomon. My research focuses on the intersection of high-performance computing (HPC), geometric data processing, and simulation. I am particularly interested in designing systems, algorithms, and data structures that accelerate complex, unstructured problems. Additionally, I work on creating abstractions and programming models that bridge the gap between high performance and ease of use.

Prior to joining MIT, I was a Senior Research Scientist at Autodesk Research in Toronto, Canada, from 2021 to 2024. In 2024, I earned my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of California, Davis, under the supervision of Professor John D. Owens, and received an M.Sc. from the same department in 2020. I obtained my B.Sc. in Marine Engineering and Naval Architecture from Alexandria University, Egypt, in 2013.
